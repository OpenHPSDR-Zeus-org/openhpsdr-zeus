
The Hermes-Lite 2 is an entirely open source and open hardware project. This includes not only the source files, but the tools used to design the Hermes-Lite as well as all production files. Full releases are found on [github](https://github.com/softerhardware/Hermes-Lite2/tree/master/hardware/hl/releases). Because this project is entirely open source and open hardware, there are several ways to realize a Hermes-Lite 2. A goal of this project is to explore and pursue new and inexpensive ways to have open hardware manufactured in small batches.

Do not be concerned if a release name includes the word beta. The Hermes-Lite 2.0 has been released starting with beta2 at the end of 2016. All beta versions will be supported in the future. The term beta is used for a functioning unit, similar to what is described [here](https://www.centercode.com/blog/2014/03/how-ibm-and-google-shaped-the-term-beta/). Recent releases have switched to append *buildN* where N is the group buy build number.

## Group Buys

From time to time, group buys are organized. To express interest in a future group buy and add your name to the list, please send mail to hermeslite at gmail.com. Also, check or post to the [Hermes-Lite Google Group](https://groups.google.com/forum/#!forum/hermes-lite).

Group buys are typically for at least 25 units. Recent group buys have had most of the Hermes-Lite 2.0 assembled and include an option to add on the N2ADR companion filter board. This is not a boxed unit and you are still expected to do a little through hole soldering and final assembly with a separate enclosure. 

About 20% is added to the price to cover a few bad or lost units. So far this has been enough to cover any accidents or field failures within 90 days of purchase, but group buys do not include a full guarantee.

### Printed Circuit Boards Only

The [release directories](https://github.com/softerhardware/Hermes-Lite2/tree/master/hardware/hl/releases) include a gerber files ready to be submitted to a PCB fabrication house. Budget PCB fabrication services may be found at [PCB shopper](https://pcbshopper.com/).

The [release directories](https://github.com/softerhardware/Hermes-Lite2/tree/master/hardware/hl/releases) also contain the files bom.standard.pdf and hermeslite.pdf. These are the BOM and schematic files used to assemble a Hermes-Lite 2. Although it is a challenging build with many surface mount components, others have built the Hermes-Lite 2 by hand. As seen from the BOM, parts cost will range from $160 to $190 dollars. Please refer to the getting started, test and build notes on the [wiki](https://github.com/softerhardware/Hermes-Lite2/wiki) for guidance with your build. You may have to refer to notes from past releases to find all the required information.

See the [interactive BOM](http://www.hermeslite.com/hl2b7-ibom.html) for help with hand assembly.
